I was born and raised in...West Bloomfield, MI, a township located in the Detroit Metropolitan Area (click the mitten below). But I recently moved from Michigan to Chicago, IL to dive head-first into the Web development industry, starting with furthering my education and experience by attending Dev Bootcamp(DBC). I decided to apply for the DBC program while I was working as an office manager at a dance studio in Michigan. A part of my responsibilities included updating and maintaining the business website, office systems, and business social media accounts, which eventually sparked my interests in Web Development.

When I'm not working... on web development which lately is not that often, I spend my time doing a multitude of different things. As you could assume from reading above, I got my job at a dance studio because I was a dancer and the owner was my teacher growing up. I began dancing (jazz, ballet, etc) when I was 5 years old and the rest is history. I danced competitively through my dance studio, my high school's dance team, and was even on my college's dance team for a bit. Besides dance, I enjoy researching and experimenting with different recipes, if I'm cooking for a group during the holidays or just myself during the week - it relaxes me. In addition to cooking, I love to travel, whether I'm going somewhere in the states or to a country on the other side of the world. I love going to places I've never been before and learning about their culture and history, I find it just fascinating. But above anything else, I love spending time with my friends and family, especially my adorable troublemaker niece (soon to be nieces!!).

After graduating in... 2007 from West Bloomfield High School, I went to college and majored in Elementary Education with emphases in Social Studies and Geography. Growing up I worked with children all the time, at summer camp, at dance, and I was great at it - so I never questioned whether or not teaching was right for me. Once I graduated from Michigan State University in 2012 with a BA in Elementary Education, I started having mixed feelings about teaching but wasn't sure what I wanted to do instead. Luckily, my old dance teacher decided to open a second location of her studio and needed an office manager. When she contacted me I happily accepted the position, the business grew tremendously, and I ended up working there for the next 3 years, and again, this is where I started growing an interest in web development.
